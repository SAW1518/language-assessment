# AI Tips:
Los 5 errores que más se repiten (y cómo arreglarlos)
1. Confusión past simple vs. present simple
Tú mismo lo identificaste, y sí, es tu punto más débil.

❌ "Today I started my day early, leave it to run" → ✅ "I went for a run"
❌ "When I back" → ✅ "When I came back" / "When I got back"
❌ "I took bad practices" (al hablar de algo que sigue pasando) → ✅ "I picked up bad habits"

Tip: Antes de empezar una historia, decide mentalmente "esto es pasado" o "esto es presente/general" y cásate con el tiempo verbal. Si arrancas en pasado, todos los verbos de esa anécdota van en pasado.
2. Falsos amigos del español
Esto te delata como hispanohablante:

❌ "commune techniques" → ✅ "common techniques"
❌ "actualizated / keep it update" → ✅ "keep updated" / "stay up to date"
❌ "learnings" (como sustantivo plural) → ✅ "knowledge" o "lessons"
❌ "personality" cuando quieres decir "perfil profesional" → ✅ "skills" o "profile"
❌ "legit data" (cuando querías decir datos reales) → ✅ "real data" / "genuine information"

3. "Too much" vs. "a lot" vs. "very"

❌ "We had to work too much" → ✅ "We had to work a lot"
❌ "My family is not too much rich" → ✅ "My family is not very wealthy" / "isn't rich"

Regla rápida: too much = demasiado (negativo, exceso). a lot = mucho (neutro). Si no es queja, usa a lot.
4. Preposiciones

❌ "meet one at month" → ✅ "meet once a month"
❌ "expertise learning English" → ✅ "expertise in English"
❌ "focus what I have to do" → ✅ "focus on what I have to do"
❌ "enter to the tech industry" → ✅ "enter the tech industry" (sin to)

5. Comparativos y "more"

❌ "is more probably" → ✅ "it's more likely"
❌ "have more expertise" está bien, pero cuidado con "most important" vs "more important"
